Wendy Whiteley's Secret Garden

Want to have a serene day out with yourself? Wendy Whiteley’s Secret Garden is secret, aloof, and a literal art-piece.

November 5, 2022 Sydney
Tripadvisor Rating 5/5
Google Rating 4.7/5

Lavender St, Lavender Bay NSW 2060, Australia

What?

A hidden garden sounds like a fascinating place. And Wendy's is precisely that. A secret public sanctuary in the middle of Sydney, more like the city’s one of many hidden jewels.  It's only a short walk from Milsons Point and North Sydney train stations and the Sydney Harbour Bridge. It is tucked away behind Luna Park in Lavender Bay.  There are high slopes and a variety of steps, yet it is free and accessible every day.

The Story

In 1970, Wendy and her late husband, the celebrated artist Brett Whiteley, established their family home in Lavender Bay and resided there for 20 years. In the weeks following Brett's passing in 1992, Wendy threw herself into the desolate area just at the base of her residence, cleaning everything she found there.  As she cleaned the area, Wendy started constructing a garden that looked like a huge painting. Wendy's gardening is not influenced by horticulture but is motivated by beauty, hue, design, elegance, and playfulness. 

Wendy says, "When I first began the garden, I knew nothing about gardening. I simply knew what I enjoyed. I've since discovered what it's like to be here. The plants, myself, and my gardeners all depend on one another.”

Did you know?

  • Wendy never asked any authority for permission to build the garden, nor did anyone forbid her.
  • Many of Brett's famous paintings of Sydney Harbour were created in this home.

What is Wendy Whiteley's Secret Garden Famous For?

The Secret Garden is famous for its rambling pathways with several sculptures. The most exciting thing about this place is that there is no physical sign indicating its whereabouts. To keep the “secret” part true, you can only go there by following Google Maps directions. 

How To Get There?

You can use the following public transport to visit this stunning garden:

  1. Buses 115, 230, and 320 will take you to Wendy Whiteley’s Secret Garden.
  • T1 train also goes to this garden.
  • From North Sydney, it's a 3-minute walk, and from Milsons Point, it will take nine minutes to get there.

Nearest Parking and Cost

Since the park is a private area, there is no official parking. However, you can park your vehicles in nearby streets.

Nearby Attractions – Make A Day Of It!

Here are the best nearby places around the secret garden:

Sydney Park (St Peters)
This park is famous for its sporting fields and is children-suitable. There is a cafe too on the premises. 
St. Peter's Presbyterian Church
The beautiful tall building works as a church. People use it as an excellent spiritual getaway.

Best Eateries Around

The following eateries are around Wendy Whiteley’s Secret Garden:

Chedi Thai
If you are looking for a vegan food place, Chedi Thai is popular in the neighbourhood.
Moorish Blue
This eatery serves all kinds of Moroccan, African, and Middle-eastern food.

Entry Price

There is no entry price to enter this garden. You can go there any time of the week, and it will be open. But because of its steeps and ground structure, we recommend visiting this place during daylight.
